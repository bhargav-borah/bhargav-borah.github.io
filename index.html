Bhargav Borah

MS student in Computer Engineering at Texas A&M University, advised by Dr. Guni Sharon.

My research focuses on offline goal-conditioned reinforcement learning. I’m drawn to its still-unrealized promise of foundational policies — an analogue to next-token prediction in NLP — and to the representation learning techniques that move us toward it.

Projects

  • MCTS for LLM Mathematical Reasoning CSCE 642

    A reasoning framework combining Monte Carlo Tree Search with Tree-of-Thought-style structured exploration and process reward signals to enhance step-by-step mathematical problem solving in LLMs. Trained a lightweight policy network via experience replay on collected MCTS trajectories using a fine-tuned DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-1.5B backbone with symbolic verification and log-likelihood intermediate rewards.

  • Circuit Development During Fine-Tuning ECEN 649

    Applied mechanistic interpretability techniques — path-patching, Desiderata-based Component Masking (DCM), and Cross-Model Activation Patching (CMAP) — to track how circuits evolve across LoRA, QLoRA, and full fine-tuning checkpoints. Showed that parameter-efficient fine-tuning preserves the base model’s circuit structure while progressively enhancing component functionality.

  • PhysicsPDEAgent: Agentic AI for PDE Solver Generation ECEN 744

    An agentic LLM framework that automatically generates, verifies, and iteratively repairs numerical PDE solvers by embedding physics constraints — conservation laws, stability conditions, energy bounds, positivity requirements — into the generation and correction loops. Evaluated across four PDE families (advection, Burgers’, Darcy flow, reaction-diffusion).

  • Continuous Thought Machine on MNIST

    Reproduced the Continuous Thought Machines (Darlow et al., 2025) architecture, which models neural synchronization and an internal temporal dimension inspired by biological cognition. Validated on MNIST, replicating the paper’s reported accuracy and prediction behavior.

  • Vision Transformer for Image Classification

    Implemented a Vision Transformer from scratch following An Image is Worth 16×16 Words (Dosovitskiy et al., 2020), including patch embedding and multi-head self-attention. Benchmarked on CIFAR-10 against CNN baselines.

  • Neural Machine Translation with Transformers

    Built an English–German NMT system from scratch following Attention Is All You Need (Vaswani et al., 2017): multi-head self-attention, positional encodings, and a full encoder–decoder pipeline with training and inference support.

  • Neural Style Transfer for Artistic Rendering

    Reproduced A Neural Algorithm of Artistic Style (Gatys et al., 2015) in PyTorch, leveraging VGG feature representations for style and content separation to render arbitrary content images in the style of reference artworks.
